The amount of protein our body needs

Posted by - Shahensha Rayees

How Much Protein Do You Need?

Anywhere from 10% to 30% of your calories should come from protein. So if your needs are 2,000 calories, that is 200-600 calories from protein or 50-175 grams. The advised dietary subsidy to prevent weakness for middle stagnant adults is 0.8 grams per kilogram of body weight. If you are overweight, your weight is adjusted before computing your protein needs to avoid overestimating. So, from this writing, we will be knowing how much protein our body requires on a daily or monthly basis. Well then, let’s have a look!

How Do You Calculate How Much Protein You Need to Eat?

Calculate protein needs by weight. Split your weight in pounds by 20 and multiply it by seven. You need just a little more than seven grams of protein for every 20 pounds of your body weight. Hence, for someone weighing 200 pounds, the protein condition will be 70 grams each day.

What If You Do Want to Use a Protein Supplement?

If you want to use a protein supplement, here is what to look for:

  • About 200 or fewer calories
  • 2 grams or less of saturated fat
  • No trans fat or partially hydrogenated oils
  • 4 grams of sugar or fewer

What Do 15-30 Grams of Protein in Whole Foods Look Like?

Eating a banana, Greek yogurt, and a hard-boiled egg will get you 19 grams of protein in moderation. A 3-ounce chicken breast with a half cup of rice and a half cup of vegetables amounts to 25 grams of protein. About 28 grams of protein come from an egg and a bean burrito with a glass of milk.

Where Does Protein Come From?

The healthful protein choices are plant-based such as soy, seeds, nuts, beans, lentils, lean meats such as skinless, white meat chicken, or turkey, a variety of fish or seafood, egg whites, or low-fat options. Manufactured foods don’t contain everything you need from food, and plants do not provide everything that should be in food.

Conclusion

Proteins are created as chains of amino acids, which then fold into unusual three-dimensional shapes, helping stabilize their system. The final folded form of protein is well adapted for its purpose. Hopefully, you got some idea about taking protein now!
